People in culture

Understanding different cultures opens our heart only toward other people and understanding them in the right way.

It is the best way to learn from each other, and learn more about ourselves too.

by Lukas Nagy, Oct 22 2021

Hungary is located in the central Europe with a diverse cultural milleau.

Throughout the history, they’ve struggled to find their voice and identity while being under various reign regimes.

Today, these struggles continue, yet Hungarians are solidly characterised by their level of hospitability, pride in cusine, language and more

Values

Who are we?

Hungarians as very warm and hospitable people. If you travel to Hungary, you experience how nice and helpful they are in many ways. As well as hospitable toward foreign turists, older generations up until Millenials are very keen on family.

They love socializing with their friends, family and attending events. It is mainly because in the past, the empasize was put on raising kids and working in the communist era.

At that time, there was no freedom of free thinking, travelling and individualistic development. The only thing Hungarians could do is children and work. This has also resulted in scepticism toward the government, and new changes overall.

Now, the government is seen as manipulative and restraining mainly because the government are abusing the power they had seized after the Velvet revolution.

Divided or connected?

Hungarians tend to be somber and nostalgic. That comes from the Trianon era when Hungary had way more land than now. For the contribution to the WW2, Hungary was scrapped off of their lands. Those were the lands with the best economic resources.

Now, majority of Hungarians look back in the past as on great times when they thrived. This is also called as the Trianon Syndrome. However, Generation Z seeks the acceptance of the events and lets go of the grudge.

Another trait of Hungarians is resiliance and adaptibility. Hungarians always found a way how to adapt under various regimes and situations. The saying “A zúrt nagykapu mellett, mindig van egy kiskapu” (“If the big gate is closed, there’s always a little gate open”) reflected it pretty much.

At one point, Hungarians were buying fridges in Vienna and drive them home, tied to the roof of the car. This was happening because in the past, there was a shortgage of home appliances.

Language

Hungarian is one of the most complex and hardest language to learn. No matter the location of the country or its neighbours.

Actually, it’s Magyar

The proper way hot to name Hungarian language is ‘Magyar’ just like Hungarians. This fact is respected though, as not many countries have the proper translation of the name.

Ah, I feel so lonely...

Magyar is completely different from all language countries and has no other it could speak to in Hungarian.

It actually comes from Asia and belongs to Finno-Ugric group family. The only countries which are related to them language wise are Finnland and Estonia.

No, they can’t talk to each other in their languages :(

Change is for the weak

As a language, Magyar remained true to its ancient roots.

It contains a whooping 68% of their etymons and original word. It did change slightly under the influence of English with 4% of their influence or kept words from Hebrew with 5%. are Finnland and Estonia.
